Common questions

Does Palaemon tell me whether it's safe to go out?

No — and that's deliberate. Palaemon compares conditions against limits you set for your own boat and experience, and tells you whether conditions are within, near, or over your limits. It is an advisory planning reference, not a safety or navigation service. Always check official forecasts and use your own judgment at sea.

Do I need to grant location access?

No. Location just makes it convenient — Palaemon fetches conditions near you while the app is open. You can instead type a coordinate or pick a point on the map. Location is never tracked or stored by us; see the privacy policy.

Why do tides or currents say "not available" for my spot?

Tide and current predictions come from real NOAA stations. If there is no station near your location, Palaemon says so honestly rather than guessing. Try a nearby harbor or inlet with a station, or check the station list on the tides screen.

Is my data backed up? Can I sync between devices?

Your vessels, trips, and limits are stored on your device and sync automatically through your private iCloud, so they appear on all devices signed into the same Apple ID. We run no servers and cannot see your data.

I found a bug or have a feature request.

Email us at jollies.ocelots8w@icloud.com with as much detail as you can, including your device model, iOS version, and roughly where you were looking (conditions load per-location).
